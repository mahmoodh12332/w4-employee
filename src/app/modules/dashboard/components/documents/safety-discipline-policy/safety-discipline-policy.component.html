Safety Discipline Policy

For Pacesetter Personnel Services’ Employees at

AmeriGas Cylinder Exchange (ACE) Facilities

All personnel (both exempt and non-exempt) working at any AmeriGas Cylinder Exchange (ACE) facility are expected to work in a safe and professional manner. The goal of this policy is to ensure that safety procedures are followed at all times and that discipline is imposed consistently across all ACE Regions and facilities. This policy applies to all personnel working at an ACE production facility. This includes both AmeriGas employees and employees retained through Pacesetter Personnel Services. Any employee not abiding by these rules as set forth below will be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment.

1. Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)

All personnel at ACE production centers must properly wear AmeriGasprovided Personal Protective Equipment in designated areas. Personal Protective Equipment includes; but is not limited too:

  • Safety Glasses
  • Face Shields
  • Static-Free Clothing and/or Shoes or Heel Grounders
  • Gloves
  • Hearing Protection

Areas designated for the specific PPE set forth above will be identified with signage and all personnel must wear the appropriate PPE at all times within this designated area. If any personnel are found inside this area without proper PPE, they will be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment.

2. Electronic Devices

Cell phones and other electronic devices (iPod, MP3, etc.) are not permitted in specific areas of the facility. These items should be left in the locker or personal vehicle of the personnel who possess them. If any individual is found inside a designated area with a cell phone or other electronic device, the individual will be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment.

3. Forklift Seatbelts

Seatbelts must be worn at all times when operating a forklift. Personnel found operating a forklift without the seat belt fastened will be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment.

Discipline

Violation of the above safety policies will result in the discipline set forth below. Management will examine each case individually and consider possible mitigating and aggravating factors. While this policy will be used as a general guide, AmeriGas reserves the right to impose discipline based upon the circumstances of each incident. For example, where an employee is determined to have acted with gross negligence, a single incident may result in employment termination.

Safety Violation

(Concerning but not limited too: Glasses, Face Shield, Electronic Devices, Seatbelts, Static-FeeZone, Gloves, Hearing Protection)

First Violation

Verbal Warning – coaching session about incident
(This warning will be documented on a Disciplinary Report)

Second Violation

Written Warning – coaching session about incident

Third Violation

Suspension – three days, no pay
(Cannot substitute paid time off)

Fourth Violation

Termination of Employment

There will be a 12-month probation period after a safety infraction warning takes place. The employee will come off safety probation after they work 12 months without any safety violations.

All locations with a union contract will abide by the discipline procedures set forth in the applicable collective bargaining agreement.
